Privacy Policy

Your notes are yours. Here's exactly what we handle and why.

Effective: June 28, 2026 Applies to: BibliaTab Notebook (Android) Developer: BibliaTab

BibliaTab Notebook is a note‑taking app. Most of what you create — notes, drawings, voice memos, to‑dos — lives on your own device. This policy explains the limited data we collect when you sign in or use cloud features, who processes it, and the control you have over it.

01 Who we are

BibliaTab Notebook ("the app", "we", "us") is an Android application published by BibliaTab ("the developer"). This policy covers the app and its optional cloud features. It does not cover third‑party services you choose to connect (such as your Google account), which are governed by their own privacy policies.

We collect only what a feature needs to work. We do not sell your data, and we do not show third‑party ads.

03 How we use your information

  • To provide core features — creating, editing, organizing, and searching your notes.
  • To authenticate you and keep your profile consistent across devices.
  • To sync and back up your content only when you enable those features.
  • To process and restore in‑app purchases through Google Play.
  • To diagnose crashes and understand which features are used, so we can improve the app.

We do not use your notes' content to build advertising profiles, and we do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone.

04 Where your data is stored

On your device

Notes, media, drawings, and to‑dos are stored locally in the app's private storage. Your app‑lock PIN is stored only as a one‑way salted hash.

Cloud sync & account (optional)

If you sign in, your account profile is handled by Google Firebase (Authentication and Firestore). If you enable sync, note data associated with your account may be stored in Firebase to keep devices in sync.

Backups to your own Google Drive (optional)

If you turn on Google Drive backup, the app writes backup files to your own Google Drive using access you grant. Those files live in your Drive, under your control — we do not keep a separate copy. You can also back up to a local folder you choose on your device.

05 Third‑party services

We rely on a small number of reputable providers to deliver these features. Each processes data under its own privacy policy:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Storage, Analytics) — accounts, optional sync, and anonymous diagnostics.
  • Google Play Billing — processes purchases; we receive only your entitlement status, never payment details.
  • Google Drive API — used only to store backups in your own Drive when you enable it.

See Google's Privacy Policy for how Google processes this data.

07 Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information: sign‑in traffic is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS), your app‑lock PIN is stored only as a salted hash, and optional app lock with PIN or biometrics adds an on‑device barrier. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to protect your data using industry‑standard safeguards.

08 Data retention & deletion

  • Trash: deleted notes stay in Trash so you can restore them, then are removed when you empty Trash.
  • On‑device data: uninstalling the app removes local notes and media from your device. Export or back up first if you want to keep them.
  • Account data: you can request deletion of your account and associated cloud data at any time (see Contact). We will delete it within 30 days, except where retention is legally required.
  • Drive backups: delete them yourself from your Google Drive whenever you wish.

09 Your rights & choices

  • Access & export: export your notes as PDF, Markdown, or JSON from Settings → Export.
  • Correct: edit your profile in the app at any time.
  • Delete: clear individual notes, empty Trash, or request full account deletion.
  • Opt out of cloud: simply don't sign in or don't enable sync/backup — the app works fully offline.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA. Contact us to exercise them.

10 Children's privacy

BibliaTab Notebook is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age of digital consent in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us personal data, contact us and we will delete it.

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Effective" date above and, where appropriate, an in‑app notice. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy.
